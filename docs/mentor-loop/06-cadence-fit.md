# 06 — Cadence, fit & the finish-Tu pivot

*Facet 6 of the Mentor Loop (#43). How the loop respects his life instead of
taxing it: fitting his real rhythm, the anti-nag restraint at the cadence level,
and the high-value handoff when Tu closes.*

> **My lane.** I own *timing and frequency* — the temporal envelope around every
> card: how often a card may fire, which days earn a lighter touch or none, how
> the monthly math-day and the Tu→next-book switch are handled. I do **not**
> decide *whether a given note contains a fireable signal* (that's #1), nor what
> a repair (#2) or bridge (#3) card *says*, nor how the open-loop banner *renders*
> (#4), nor what the book skeleton *is* (#5). I consume those and gate them in
> time. Seams are named at the end; I describe them, I don't design them.

---

## The story

> **As the learner**, I want the loop to move at the speed of my actual
> practice — quiet on a distracted weeknight, patient through the days I don't
> open it at all, and present at the beats where threads actually go stale
> (Sunday, the monthly math-day, and the day I finish a book) — **so that** it
> stays the rare, trusted voice that catches the step I'm about to abandon,
> instead of a daily chore that taxes a hobby I've kept alive for ten months
> without any app at all.

**The job.** He studies alone, for love, on a rhythm nobody enforces. The loop's
value is *direction at the right moment* (facets 1–4). But direction has a cost
the moment it arrives too often, on the wrong day, or as a guilt-pile. This facet
is the **governor**: it ensures the magic of facets 1–5 is spent sparingly enough
to stay magic. Its success is mostly invisible — measured in the cards it
*suppresses*.

**The moment.** There is no moment he reaches for *this* facet — that's the point.
It never manufactures a trip. It rides the two surfaces the product already owns
(the bottom of tonight's synthesized note; the open-loop banner on the record
page) and decides, silently, **how loud to be** given the day in front of it.

   and 06-21 was a *Sunday*, his "scheduled" Hatcher day. The loop that shames a
   missed day, or that assumes Sunday always happens, is wrong about his life.

## Acceptance criteria — what "done *and good*" looks like

**A1 · Silence is the default cadence.** Across a normal week, most days end with
**no card**. A card is an *event*, not a daily fixture. If the loop fired
something every night, the criterion fails even if every card were individually
good — ubiquity is the failure. (Anchor: of the 10 logged sessions, a healthy
loop speaks on maybe two — the 06-28 bluff, perhaps the determinant bridge — and
is silent on the other eight.)

**A2 · One *new* card per day, not per note.** On the 06-20 math-day (4 notes),
the loop surfaces **at most one** new card for the *day*, not one per session. The
other sessions synthesize normally, cardless. The per-day budget holds even when
sessions span the calendar boundary of a single study arc.

**A3 · A light/distracted day earns a lighter nudge or none — detected from what
he *says*, not only the tier.** On 06-24 he was *"distracted… 15 minutes… an hour
short"* — yet the magnitude metadata scored it **standard**. "Done and good" means
the loop does **not** assign fresh homework on that day. (See the open question on
why `density_tier` alone is insufficient.)

**A4 · Gaps produce nothing — no shame, ever.** After 06-21/22/23 (a 3-day gap
including a skipped Sunday), the loop's return on 06-24 carries the *prior* open
loop forward unchanged and says nothing about the absence. No "you missed 3
days," no streak-break, no catch-up pile.

**A5 · No push, no interruption, no gamification.** Cards are only ever *seen when
he opens the app on his own rhythm* (pull, not push). There are no notifications
between sessions, no streak counters, no points or badges. Frequency is capped by
the surface itself: he can't be nagged by a thing that only speaks when he's
already looking.

**A6 · The open-loop backlog stays small enough to be a help, not a guilt list.**
Live open loops are capped (small N). When the cap is reached the loop *stops
minting new ones* until some close — it never lets the banner grow into a
reproachful to-do pile. (Without this, the 06-26 form, the 06-27 unverified
proof, and the 06-28 bluff would already be three simultaneous live loops by
month's end.)

**A7 · "I'll ask Sunday" is honored at the next *real* session in that track —
not the calendar date.** The repair close (#2's wording) creates a *checkpoint*.
If the promised Sunday is itself a gap (as 06-21 was), the check waits for the
next actual Hatcher session and re-raises there. It never fires a nag on a day he
didn't show up.

**A8 · The math-day pre-loads; it doesn't pepper.** Heading into a math-day, the
loop's job is to make the accumulated open-loops *ready as an agenda* (via the
existing banner, #4's surface), so he doesn't cold-start the way he did on 06-20
(*"I didn't read it well in the theorem itself"*). It curates the backlog at the
front; it does **not** add four fresh cards across the day.

**A9 · When Tu finishes, dangling threads are audited and handed off — none die
silently.** At the Tu→next-book switch, the loop surfaces a one-time audit of Tu's
open threads (the 06-26 level-set form, the 06-27 *"not sure about correctness"*
Lie-group proof) and hands the now-closed book to the **Recall closed-book
keep-alive** (phase-2). Repair/bridge cards for Tu retire — a closed book has no
"next action" to prescribe.

## Design decisions

**D1 · The budget unit is the *study arc*, not the note.** A "day" for budgeting
is really a contiguous study arc (the four 06-20 sessions are one arc; a single
06-24 session is one arc). One net-new card per arc. This is how "one card max"
survives contact with a 4-session math-day.

**D2 · Cadence is a *gate over* detection, not a second detector.** #1 decides a
note *contains* a fireable signal and how confident it is. This facet then asks
three cadence questions and may **veto or defer**: *Is today a light/distracted
day? Have I already spent today's card? Is the live-loop backlog full?* A "yes"
to any turns a fireable signal into silence-for-now. Detection proposes; cadence
disposes. (This keeps the hard rule "silence when unsure" — cadence only ever
makes the loop *quieter*, never louder.)

**D3 · Urgency tiers set who waits.** A **repair** (an abandoned crux — 06-28) is
time-sensitive: catch it *the night of* or lose the moment. A **bridge** (#3,
delight not homework — the determinant chain) is *not* perishable; it can wait for
a richer beat (a Sunday, the math-day) rather than spending a thin Wednesday's
single card. So when both are eligible on the same arc, the repair wins the slot
and the bridge defers. Cadence owns this triage of *which* eligible card gets the
day's one slot.

**D4 · The checkpoint follows the *track*, not the clock.** "I'll ask Sunday" is
correct for a *Hatcher* thread because Hatcher is what he does on Sundays. A *Tu*
hedge's natural checkpoint is his next Tu session, phrased accordingly ("I'll
check next session"). The literal word "Sunday" is a Hatcher-specific surface
form of the general rule: *re-raise at the next session in this thread's book*.

**D5 · The math-day's restraint is "carry, don't add."** The monthly 4–5h day is
the one beat where *closing* old loops is the agenda. The loop's contribution is
pre-loading the backlog (A8) and, optionally, a single end-of-day audit of what
closed — never a fresh card after each of the four sessions.

**D6 · The finish-Tu pivot is a *flagship*, not a fallback.** Being ~80pp from the
end of Tu is the highest-value tutoring moment in the whole arc: the instant a
book's open threads are about to be orphaned. The loop turns the switch into a
deliberate audit + handoff, not a silent drop.

## Open questions

**Q1 · How is a "light/distracted" day detected, given the metadata lies?** This
is the sharpest open question, and it *corrects a claim in the winner doc*. The
winner says *"the mentor reads magnitude and doesn't assign homework on a short
day,"* citing 06-24. But 06-24's `magnitude.density_tier` is **standard** (3 pages,
605 transcript chars) — the metadata would *not* have caught it. The real signal
was *verbal*: *"distracted… 15 minutes… an hour short."* So light-day detection
must read his **spoken self-report of effort**, with `density_tier` as a coarse
secondary prior, not the primary. **Who extracts that verbal cue — #1 (as part of
the signal taxonomy) or this facet?** Proposed: #1 surfaces a "self-reported
light/distracted" flag alongside its fire signal; cadence consumes it. (Seam to
#1.) Default when ambiguous: treat as light → no new homework.

**Q2 · How is a math-day known *in advance* of session 1?** He only announces it
*inside* session 1 (*"Today is my math day"*) — too late to pre-load before he
records. Options: (a) lean entirely on the existing banner — the backlog is
*already* there every time he opens to record, so the math-day "pre-load" is
mostly *not adding noise* rather than a special pre-fetch; (b) monthly-cadence
inference (it recurs ~monthly); (c) detect his verbal announcement and shift to
math-day restraint for the *rest* of the day. Proposed default: (a)+(c) — no
calendar magic; the banner carries the agenda, and the announcement flips the
day's budget to "carry, don't add."

**Q3 · What is the live-loop cap, and how do stale loops retire?** A3/A6 require a
cap, but the number and the expiry policy are open. Do loops age out silently
after K sessions untouched? Get bundled ("3 older threads")? Proposed: a small cap
(~3 live), oldest-untouched loops fold into a single muted "older threads" line in
the banner rather than expiring outright — preserves the audit value for the
math-day without growing the guilt surface. (Backlog *rendering* is #4's; the
*policy* is mine.)

**Q4 · How is "Tu is finishing" detected to trigger the pivot?** Either from his
words (*"finished the book," "last exercise," "moving on"* — note he already says
*"don't spoil the last exercise"* on 06-25) or from the book skeleton (#5) knowing
Tu's extent and his notes mapping near the end. Proposed: skeleton-driven primary
(position ≥ ~90% of Tu's anchors) with verbal confirmation as the trigger. (Seam
to #5; this facet consumes "near the end," it doesn't build the skeleton.)

## Edge cases & failure modes

**E1 · The math-day fires four cards.** *Cause:* per-note budgeting. *Handled by:*
D1 (per-arc budget) + A2 — the four 06-20 sessions yield ≤1 new card.

**E2 · A genuinely hard day is misread as slacking, or a distracted day as full
effort.** 06-24 is the live example (metadata "standard," reality "distracted").
*Handled by:* A3 + Q1 — read the verbal cue; when ambiguous, err toward *lighter*.
The asymmetry is deliberate: a missed nudge costs nothing; a homework assignment
on a bad day costs trust.

**E3 · A multi-day gap.** 06-21/22/23, including a skipped Sunday. *Handled by:*
A4 — silence, loops wait, no shame, no "Sunday check" fired into the void.

**E4 · The "I'll ask Sunday" promise lands on a skipped Sunday.** *Cause:* the
checkpoint is calendar-bound. *Handled by:* A7/D4 — the check follows the *next
real session in that track*, not the date. The 06-28 Van Kampen bluff's "I'll ask
Sunday" would re-raise at the following actual Hatcher session.

**E5 · The backlog becomes a guilt-pile.** Five live threads → the banner reads as
a reproach. *Handled by:* A6/Q3 — cap + fold-into-"older threads."

**E6 · He does the exercise on paper and never reports it.** *Cause:* the loop
can't see paper. *Handled by:* treat "can't tell" as silence (the loop's universal
default), and let closure be *inferred* from the next note or the one-tap — that
inference is #4's; cadence just guarantees the unresolved loop doesn't *re-nag* in
the meantime, it waits.

**E7 · Tu finishes mid-week with two open threads.** *Cause:* a book-switch
orphans its loops. *Handled by:* A9/D6 — a one-time audit (06-26 form, 06-27
unverified proof) + handoff to Recall, instead of the threads silently dying when
the daily track moves to a new book.

**E8 · Cards become wallpaper.** *Cause:* firing too often erodes the signal-value
even of good cards. *Handled by:* A1 — silence-as-default is itself a cadence
constraint, not just a detection one; the scarcity *is* the value.

## Grounded examples

**Math-day collapse (06-20).** Four sessions: cold-start (*"I didn't read it well
in the theorem itself"*), then *"I think this is the whole point, right?"*, then
*"Still figuring it out"*, then a resolution (*"Okay, I understood why…"*). A
per-note loop fires 4 cards. **This facet fires zero new cards mid-day** — the
banner already carries his open threads as the day's agenda — and at most one
**end-of-day audit** noting what closed and what's still dangling for next time.

**Distracted weeknight (06-24).** Metadata: *standard*. Reality: *"I was distracted
full time, 15 minutes from the session… an hour a bit short."* He still left an
honest open thread (*"I have not finished the proof yet, so don't spoil"*). **This
facet:** carry the existing loop forward, assign **no new homework**, stay silent.
The thread waits for a day he's actually present.

**The Sunday checkpoint that wasn't (06-21).** A repair promised "I'll ask Sunday."
06-21 (Sunday) is a gap. **This facet:** no nag fires on 06-21; the checkpoint
holds and re-raises at the next real Hatcher session (here, 06-28) — patient, not
punitive.

**The finish-Tu pivot (the ~80pp horizon).** Daily Tu carries live threads — the
06-26 regular-level-set non-vanishing form (*"a bit a weird problem"*) and the
06-27 Lie-group parallelizability proof he flagged *"not really sure about
correctness."* When Tu closes, **this facet:** surfaces a one-time *"before you
start the next book, three Tu threads are still open"* audit, then **hands the
closed book to Recall** so ten months of manifolds is kept alive by spaced
keep-alive — because once Tu is shut there is no "next action in the book" for a
repair card to point at. Mentor owns *"don't let the live chapter close with its
crux unproven"*; Recall owns *"don't let the closed book evaporate."* The switch
is where one passes the baton to the other.

## Seams (named, not designed)

- **← #1 Detection & restraint.** I receive: *is there a fireable signal, and how
  confident?* — plus (proposed, Q1) a **self-reported-light/distracted flag**
  extracted from the transcript. I return nothing to detection; I gate its output
  in time (defer/suppress per D2). The seam: *detection decides whether a note can
  speak; I decide whether today is a day to.*

- **← #2 Repair card.** I receive the card and its **"I'll ask Sunday" close**. I
  own when "Sunday" actually arrives (A7/D4) and re-surface the checkpoint at the
  next real session in that track. I don't write the close; I schedule it.

- **← #3 Bridge card.** I receive an eligible bridge. Because it's delight, not
  homework (non-perishable), I may **defer** it to a richer beat rather than spend
  a thin day's slot on it (D3). I don't author the bridge; I choose its moment.

- **↔ #4 The loop close.** #4 owns the banner *render* and the one-tap closure
  states. I own the **backlog policy behind it** — the live-loop cap, the
  math-day pre-load ordering, the fold-to-"older threads" rule (A6/A8/Q3). The
  seam: *#4 draws the banner; I decide how full it's allowed to get and what's in
  it.*

- **← #5 Book skeleton & grounding.** I consume the skeleton's knowledge of **a
  book's extent and his position in it** to know Tu is *near the end* (Q4/A9). I
  don't build or maintain the skeleton; I read "≥~90% through Tu" off it to fire
  the pivot.

- **→ Recall closed-book keep-alive (#42, phase-2 complement).** At the Tu finish I
  **hand off** the closed book + its audited open threads. I define the *trigger*
  (Tu closes), the *audit* (curate dangling threads at the switch), and the
  *handoff* — I do **not** design Recall's spacing, scheduling, or quiz internals.
  That is the phase-2 product; here I only build the door it walks through.

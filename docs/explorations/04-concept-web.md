# 04 — The Concept Web

*Lens: tools-for-thought (Matuschak / Roam / Obsidian-graph). The unit is the **idea**, not the page, not the day, not the book.*

## The bet

This product **is his personal concept graph**: every idea he's studied is a node, his notes hang off it, and the edges are the connections between ideas — especially the ones that **secretly link his two tracks**. It exists to do the one thing he says he loves and the one thing nothing in his life currently does for him: **connect dots**. Not "where am I in the book" (that's the Atlas) and not "review this Tuesday" (that's the Recall Engine) — *"these two things you studied are the same thing, and you didn't notice."*

## The job & the moment

His practice is **two siloed tracks that never talk**. The data is stark: weekdays are Tu / smooth manifolds (Wed–Fri: orientation, differential forms, regular level sets), and the weekend burst — a Saturday with **four sessions** — plus Sunday is Hatcher / algebraic topology (van Kampen, quotient groups, fundamental group). Two books, two days-of-week, two universes. **But the ideas leak across constantly, and he can't see it**, because his notes are a flat daily stream sorted by time.

The moment he reaches for this is **not daily and not when stuck** — it's the **reflective beat**: end of a session, the Sunday wind-down, the monthly 5h math-day when he has room to think structurally. It's the "huh, wait —" moment. The Concept Web manufactures those moments instead of waiting for them.

## The experience / the aha

He opens the app and sees a **graph of concepts**, not a list of days. He clicks **`determinant`** and the web lights up everything that touches it — and here is the real, in-his-corpus aha, drawn straight from his notes of the last 8 days:

- **Jun 19 (Tu):** orientation defined by the *sign of a change-of-basis determinant*.
- **Jun 20 (alg-top day):** the *determinant homomorphism* `det: GL(m) → ℝ*`, with **SL(m) as its kernel**.
- **Jun 24–25 (Tu):** orientation again, now via the *Jacobian determinant* and its multiplicativity.
- **Jun 26 (Tu):** a *regular level set* of a smooth map is an orientable submanifold.
- **Jun 27 (Tu):** *every Lie group is orientable.*

The web draws the edge he never drew himself: **SL(m) is the kernel of the determinant (algebra) *and* a regular level set of the determinant (manifolds) *and* a Lie group, hence orientable.** Four notes, two books, one object. The app surfaces a banner:

> **You've now touched the determinant from four sides — orientation, group homomorphism, level set, Lie group. They're the same map. Here's how your notes connect.**

That is the magic moment: not information, **recognition**. The serendipitous *"you've now touched both sides of X"* that a flat daily diary structurally cannot give him.

The other live bridges already sitting in his corpus, ready to be drawn:

- **Quotient-by-an-equivalence** — orientation is an *equivalence class of ordered bases* (Tu, Jun 19/25); cosets and quotient groups are *equivalence classes* (alg-top, Jun 20). Same abstract move, two tracks.
- **Local-to-global gluing** — a *Lebesgue-number argument* chops a loop along an open cover (Jun 20); *gluing local forms on overlaps* builds a global volume form (Jun 26). Same machine.
- **The bridge he hasn't crossed yet (a *typed* edge, not a co-occurrence):** he has the exterior derivative, wedge product, and top-degree forms on one bank, and the fundamental group and homotopy on the other. **de Rham cohomology is the river between them** — differential forms *computing* topological invariants. The web marks this as a `bridges →` edge with a "prerequisites met" badge: *you already own both sides; one definition connects them.*

## MVP

Concepts already exist — `synthesis.concepts` is extracted on every note today. The thin slice:

1. **Nodes** = the distinct extracted concepts across his corpus (dedup near-duplicates: "Jacobian determinant" / "change-of-basis determinant" → `determinant`).
2. **Edges, two kinds only:** (a) *co-occurrence* — concepts that appear together or recur across notes (free, already in the data); (b) a handful of **typed bridges** — `prereq`, `generalizes`, `bridges` — generated by one Opus pass over the concept set, prompted specifically to **find cross-track links**.
3. **One screen:** a clickable graph; clicking a node shows the notes attached to it. **One hero feature:** a "**Bridges**" panel that lists the cross-track connections, newest first, with the one-line "why these are the same."

If the only thing the MVP ever shipped was the **Bridges panel** — "this week, your two tracks met at: the determinant; quotient-by-equivalence" — it would already deliver the bet. The graph is the beautiful surface; the bridge is the product.

## Why this angle wins

The other six all keep his two tracks **separate**. The Atlas draws two book-maps side by side. Recall and Streak operate per-track. The Mentor and Companion react to one note at a time. **Only the Concept Web treats his whole practice as one mind and finds the wires between the rooms.** That is the literal thing he told us he values — *connecting dots across math* — and it is the one capability that gets *more valuable the longer he studies* and the more tracks he runs. When he finishes Tu (~80 pages out) and picks a new topic, every node he already owns becomes a prerequisite-anchor for the next book: the web tells him *where the new topic plugs into what he already knows.* It turns "starting a new book" from a blank page into an extension of an existing graph.

## Risks / why it might fail

- **The hairball.** Tools-for-thought graphs notoriously decay into an unreadable pile of dots nobody opens. **Mitigation: the graph is the garnish, the Bridges *list* is the meal.** Lead with the typed cross-track links as prose; let the graph be optional eye-candy.
- **Two weeks is a thin web.** A 10-note corpus barely connects. But the bridges above are *already real today* — the determinant chain is in the data right now — so the aha lands on day one and compounds. Still, this product's payoff curve is the steepest of the seven: weakest at week 2, strongest at month 12.
- **False edges erode trust.** A bogus "these connect!" from a sloppy LLM pass is worse than silence — for a mathematician, a wrong link is offensive. **Precision over recall:** show *few, certain* bridges, each defensible from his own notes, or show none.
- **It informs but may not re-engage.** The learner's stated north star is a *reinforcing loop back into the book*. The Concept Web is gloriously contemplative but doesn't itself say "go do exercise 9.3" — that's the Mentor's job. This bet wins on *insight and delight*, and trusts that delight is what makes a hobbyist come back. If his real need is behavioral nudging, the Streak or Mentor beats it.

## Fit to his practice

- **Tu / Hatcher split** is the *reason this product exists*: two tracks siloed by day-of-week (Tu Wed–Fri, alg-top Sat/Sun) whose ideas secretly converge. The web is the only thing that spans them.
- **Cadence:** the daily session *feeds* the graph (every note drops in nodes); he *reads* it on the **reflective beats** — Sunday wind-down, the monthly 5h math-day — exactly the "now and then" rhythm he already admits to, except now there's a payoff for returning.
- **Finishing Tu (~80pp out):** the web turns the scary "pick a new topic" into "plug a new book into the graph you already own" — it'll show which of his existing nodes are prerequisites for the candidate.
- **2-week corpus:** thin but *already bridged* — the determinant chain is real today, so the aha works now and gets better every single day he logs. This is the product that most rewards his 10-months-and-counting consistency.

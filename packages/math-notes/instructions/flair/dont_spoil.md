kind: flair
label: "Don't spoil"
description: "You're mid-exercise: keep the cleaned-up notes, but don't finish or reveal the answer to anything you left unfinished."
---
The learner is partway through one or more exercises and has explicitly asked NOT to be spoiled. Faithfully reconstruct and tidy the mathematics they actually wrote — fix transcription noise and obvious slips in the parts they completed — but DO NOT complete, continue, extend, or reveal the solution to any exercise, proof, or computation they left unfinished. Stop exactly where they stopped: do not supply the missing steps, do not state the final result or concluding claim, and do not add steps they had not yet reached. If a proof or derivation trails off, render it up to that point and leave it open. It is better to under-deliver and leave something open than to spoil it.
